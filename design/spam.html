Spam!

HCI Civic Engagement Mobile UX Badges Prototyping

CS 220: Human Computer Interaction

We want to drive student interest in civic engagement on campus through making the activity of student government and org eboards more transparent. Our design will allow all students on campus to gain easy access to voting on important matters on campus and notify them when there are issues that may affect them. Our app will also feature a wide selection of badges that students can earn for engaging in various activities on the app (including voting for the first time, attending an event in person, etc.).

The development of this app concept and development included researching needs, creating personas, task analysis, multiple wireframe ideation and designs, and prototyping. We used trained AI models developed to help us analyze our data along the way, and exercised different methods of executing our idea using the platforms Figma, Balsamiq, and coding languages HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

Research & Process

Introduction / Problem

Spam! addresses low visibility into student government & org activity and the friction to act. The app makes voting and issue awareness accessible, and motivates participation with badges for actions like first vote, event attendance, etc.

User Analysis — Interview Takeaways
  • Students forget events and attend only a few; they feel overwhelmed by org participation.
  • Traditional promo (e.g., posters) is time-consuming; digital comms can feel spammy.
  • Opportunity: app can organize events, facilitate comms, and be social/personalized.
Personas
  • Wendy — Busy junior & org president who needs better event organization, promotion, and leadership recruiting beyond email lists.
  • Emily — First-year exploring campus, wants low-friction ways to discover orgs and manage stress while finding “her place.”
  • Sofia — Sophomore leader pursuing more eboard roles and greater campus impact while picking up knowledge from graduating leaders.
Domain Analysis

Stakeholders include students, faculty, alumni; tools include RSVP platforms, org sites, 25Live, WeEngage, Google Calendar/Forms, Apple Reminders; and physical resources like campus spaces and org positions.

Scenarios
  • Event Searching: Filter for civic events and perks (e.g., free food), RSVP, and attend with friends.
  • Org Follow-up: Find club pages, read/ask FAQs, and contact members.
  • Eboard & Elections: Discover elections, upload SOIs, and cast votes.
Design Directions & Review Takeaways
  • Voting tab with current/past elections; results revealed after voting (with organizer discretion).
  • Events tab filters for civic engagement; sort by date/time and audience scope.
  • Helpful resources (e.g., voter registration); profile badges for engagement; easy sharing.
Wireframe Rationale & Risky Interactions
  • Keep layouts block-based and consistent; isolate complex flows on their own pages.
  • Badges: Earned for actions; sash UI with “pin top badge.”
  • Voting Confirmation: Explicit “Vote Send” action and clear feedback; separate tallies for parallel elections.
Final Prototype & Visual System
  • Color system derived from the Spam can (contrasting yellows/blues), used to guide navigation and support accessibility.
  • White space creates clear borders for dense information.
