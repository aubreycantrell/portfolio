BakeBot

Voice UX ASR NLP Hands-free Timers

Resources: Voiceflow  |  Usability Testing – Round 1  |  Usability Testing – Round 2

Research & Process

Introduction / Problem

Home baking is messy and time-sensitive. Switching between wet hands and a device causes missed steps, misread amounts, and poorly timed actions. BakeBot is a hands-free voice assistant that clarifies steps, manages timers, and handles conversions/substitutions so bakers focus on making—not juggling.

Needs & Assumptions
  • Needs: clear step-by-step guidance; conversions; substitutions; temp & scale help; integrated timers; pacing (“repeat”, “back”, “what’s next?”).
  • Assumptions: user can speak/hear device; pantry & basic tools available; recipes are decomposable into steps and slots.
  • Non-goals (v1): nutrition analysis; direct oven control; long-term meal planning.

Stakeholder / Domain Analysis

Primary: novice & time-constrained bakers. Secondary: experienced bakers, parents baking with kids, creators/publishers. Ecosystem: online recipes, video walkthroughs, kitchen timers, smart speakers.
User Analysis — Interview / Survey Takeaways
  • Strong need for clear, sequential instructions with reminders (preheat, rest).
  • Hands-free control is essential; avoid scrolling/tapping mid-step.
  • Pain points: conversions, substitutions, parallel timers, and unclear terminology.
Scenarios
  • Hands-free walkthrough: “next”, “repeat”, “slower”, definitions; BakeBot tracks progress & timers.
  • Substitution: “No buttermilk — options?” → ratios with texture trade-offs.
  • Conversions & scaling: “Double the recipe”, “convert 200g to cups”.
Design Directions & Concept Generation
  • Voice-first chat with step awareness, slot elicitation, and dialog repair.
  • Quick helpers for preheat/timers/scale; concise confirmations.
  • Optional screen companion to visualize current step & timers.
Concept Down-selection — Why Voice First
  • Best fit for messy-hands context; minimizes context switching.
  • Natural slot confirmation & recovery in dialog.
  • Extensible to small displays without depending on them.
Dialog / Interaction Model
  • Intents: navigate steps; define terms; set/manage timers; convert/scale; substitute; summarize step.
  • Slots: ingredient, quantity, unit, temperature, duration, tool/equipment, step index.
  • Policies: confirm critical values; ask for missing info; short, actionable responses.
Risky Interactions & Mitigations
  • ASR/noise: prefer short utterances; confirm on critical values; easy correction (“actually 1½ cups”).
  • Parallel timers: named timers (“rest”, “bake”, “cool”); distinct alerts; quick status checks.
  • Ambiguous subs: provide best option + trade-offs; avoid overwhelming choice lists.
Prototype
  • Built in Voiceflow with multi-intent dialog, slot elicitation, confirmations, and timers.
  • Supports step navigation, conversions/scaling, substitutions, and timer management.
  • Web embed for quick trials (“Test your agent”).
Usability Testing & Iteration
  • Shorter responses + explicit next actions improved flow.
  • More resilient recovery prompts when slots missing/unclear.
  • Standardized units/rounding; added named timers.
Future Work
  • Recipe import/step parsing; richer substitution knowledge; personalization (skill level, units).
  • Optional IoT integrations (read-only timers first).
